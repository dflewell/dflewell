The promise
"Nothing touches your inbox

There is no background process reading your mail. The tool runs only when you click "Start my morning." No button press, no activity.

What happens when you click

It proposes. You decide.

Pull overnight mail→ Sort into 4 buckets→ Draft suggested replies→ Show you a summary

At this stage it only proposes. It does not send, delete, or move anything. You review everything before any action is ever taken.

Where your data goes

Three places. No more.

1 · Gmail

Your mail stays in your own account. We request read access only.

2 · Claude API

Each message's text is sent to sort it — in transit only, and not used to train models.

3 · Your private database

A categorized copy, yours alone — no sharing with anyone else I work with — with the email bodies encrypted.

How long · how to delete

You can erase it all in one click.

  • The categorized history is kept so you get a durable, searchable archive — the goal is that you never lose an email again, so nothing is auto-deleted.
  • On a single request, I delete your entire database and the stored access token.
  • You can also cut off my access yourself in your Google security settings in about 30 seconds.
  • I confirm any deletion in writing.
The access I'll request

Read-and-modify — but I only ever propose.

The modify permission is there for later, only if and when you decide you want the tool to move or file mail for you. Today, at the starting rung, it reads and suggests — nothing more.
