How it goes

Nothing gets deleted without you. Every step below either reads or copies — you make the calls on what stays.

  1. Rescue Carbonite
    Confirm the old computer's backup is still there and safe, then restore it — including the files you've been trying to track down.
  2. Inventory everything
    Build one master list of every file across all four services — name, type, size, date — so you can search once instead of hunting in four places.
  3. Design your folders
    Agree on a simple structure that matches how you look for things — Personal vs. Work, kept shallow and easy to remember.
  4. Clear duplicates
    The list flags copies of the same file. You review and confirm which to keep — photos and videos first, since that's where the clutter is.
  5. Settle into Google Drive
    Move the keepers into your new structure, then retire the extra accounts once they're empty. It's yours to run from here.

For our first session

One afternoon gets us through the rescue and the full inventory. A few things to have ready:

  • Your MacBook, charged.
  • Logins for Carbonite, Dropbox, Google Drive, and your Apple ID — plus your phone for any verification codes.
  • An external hard drive if you have one (a safe place to land the Carbonite files).
  • A short list of the files you've been searching for — we'll confirm we've got them.
  • A sense of how you look for things: by client, by project, by year? No wrong answer — it shapes your folders.
⏳

One thing that's time-sensitive: a Carbonite backup tied to a computer you no longer use can eventually be purged. That's why it's step one — the sooner we log in and confirm it's safe, the better.

You don't need to prepare anything technical — just show up with the logins. I'll handle the tools; you decide what to keep. By the end, everything lives in one place you control.
